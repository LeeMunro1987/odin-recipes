Scouse

What is Scouse?

Scouse is a type of stew, typically made from chunks of meat, usually beef or lamb, potatoes and onion.

Ingredients

  • 4 tablespoons olive oil
  • 700g diced chuck steak
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 sprig of thyme
  • 400g diced onion (cut into 1cm pieces)
  • 350g diced swede (cut into 1.5cm cubes)
  • 350g carrots (cut into 1.5cm cubes)
  • 600g peeled and diced potatoes (cut into 1.5cm cubes)
  • 500ml bitter
  • 1.2 litres beef stock

Method

  1. Heat a little oil in a large saucepan over a medium heat (gas mark 4/180C/350F) for about 1 minute.

  2. Add 700g diced chuck steak and stir occasionally to prevent sticking. Cook until meat is evenly browned on all sides. Season with salt and pepper.

  3. Add the onions and cook until soft.

  4. Add the bitter and boil until the liquid has reduced by half.

  5. Add the diced and peeled carrot, swede and half of the potato, followed by bay leaves and the sprig of thyme.

  6. Then add in the beef stock, bring to the boil and simmer for 30 minutes.

  7. Add in the rest of the potato and simmer for an hour and a half until the meat is tender. Check the seasoning and serve.
